A quick, honest note on "deep sea": True deep-sea (offshore) fishing means running 9+ miles into the Gulf for snapper, grouper, and larger species. Of these three, only Hubbard's Marina runs genuine offshore trips. Happy Hour and Wish Upon A Fish are private inshore/nearshore charters — calmer water, closer in. Both are wonderful; they're just different days. If Marla has her heart set on the deep blue water, point her to option 03.

Before You Board

What's aboard, and what to pack

Included on your trip

  • Fishing license & permits — covered by the boat, no need to buy your own
  • Bait — dead/cut bait provided; live bait available to purchase
  • Rods, reels & tackle (private charters include; Hubbard's rents rods at $9.95)
  • Experienced local captain & crew to bait, net, and guide
  • Fish cleaning at the dock on return

What to bring along

  • Reef-safe sunscreen & a hat — the Gulf sun is stronger on the water
  • Sunglasses (polarized) & a light layer for the ride out
  • Snacks & water (Hubbard's has a full galley with food & drinks aboard)
  • Soft-soled, non-marking shoes and a small cooler for your catch
  • Cash for crew gratuity & any onboard extras
Good to Know

Questions, answered

Inshore/nearshore trips stay in calmer bay and coastal waters close to land — great for redfish, snook, trout, and an easy, comfortable ride (Happy Hour, Wish Upon A Fish). Deep-sea/offshore runs 9+ miles into the Gulf for snapper, grouper, and larger species, on bigger, more stable boats (Hubbard's). Deep sea means more time traveling and potentially bigger swells, but access to the "blue water" fish.
